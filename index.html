Chicago's Shifting Geography of Violent Crime

With 2.7 million residents as of 2016, Chicago is the third largest city in the United States. This map provides a glimpse at how crime rates varied over Chicago's 77 neighborhoods in 2015. Shown here are five crimes that are defined by the FBI as violent crimes: assault, burglary, robbery, sexual assault, and homicide. These are crimes in which an offender uses or threatens force upon a victim. Burglary is included here because it is often regarded as a violent crime by police and sentenced as such. All crime types are listed as rates per 10,000 people.
